Most Used Recycling Symbols

1. The Recycling Triangle

The most recognizable recycling symbol, featuring three arrows forming a triangle. It indicates that the item is recyclable and can be processed to create new products. This symbol is often seen on containers and packaging.

2. Plastic #1 (PET or PETE)

Represented by a triangle with the number 1 inside it. This symbol is used for polyethylene terephthalate (PET), commonly found in water bottles and food containers. It is widely accepted in recycling programs.

3. Plastic #2 (HDPE)

This symbol features a triangle with the number 2 inside it, indicating high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Found in items like milk jugs and detergent bottles, HDPE is commonly recyclable.

4. Plastic #3 (PVC)

Represented by a triangle with the number 3 inside it, this symbol denotes polyvinyl chloride (PVC). Commonly used in pipes and some food packaging, PVC is less frequently recycled.

5. Plastic #4 (LDPE)

This symbol, with the number 4 inside the triangle, stands for low-density polyethylene (LDPE). Used in grocery bags and some food wraps, LDPE is not always accepted in curbside recycling programs.

6. Plastic #5 (PP)

Represented by a triangle with the number 5 inside it, this symbol indicates polypropylene (PP). Found in yogurt containers and straws, PP is widely recyclable but not universally accepted.

7. Plastic #6 (PS)

The triangle with the number 6 inside it represents polystyrene (PS). Commonly used in disposable cutlery and foam cups, PS is often not accepted in recycling programs due to its low recycling rates.

8. Plastic #7 (Other)

This symbol, with the number 7 inside it, covers other plastics not categorized by the first six types. It includes materials like polycarbonate and bioplastics, which are often not widely recyclable.

9. Glass

Represented by a symbol of a broken glass item, or by a person holding a bottle. This indicates that the glass is recyclable. It is usually sorted by color and can be processed into new glass containers.

10. Aluminium

Typically found on things like tin foil or cans, this indicates that the metal used in the product is made from aluminium and can be recycled.

11. Waste Electronics

This symbol explains that you should not place electrical items in your waste bin. Electrical items can be recycled a number of ways including via retailers and at Recycling Centres.
